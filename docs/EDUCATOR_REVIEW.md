# Educator usability review protocol

This is a protocol, not a completed review. No educator identity, quotation,
approval, validation, pilot, or adoption claim exists until consent and the
session record say otherwise. Students and real learner records are not needed.

## Current execution status

As of 2026-07-17, the consented usability session has **not been conducted**.
Three outreach messages were sent by the project owner. No educator response,
consent, session, or approval has been recorded. The release therefore makes no
pilot, educator-review, validation, adoption, or usability result claim.

## Participant and consent

Recruit one consenting KSU instructor, teaching assistant, tutor, or teacher
educator. Before the session, obtain private consent for participation. Obtain
separate, optional consent for each public use: README, screenshots, video,
Devpost description, and quotations. Store identity and raw feedback outside the
repository.

## Materials

Use the six fixed records in `data/evaluation/manifest.v1.json`. They are
educator-authored project fixtures, not student work. Reset the product before
the session and confirm no production learner token or unrelated case is visible.

## Procedure

For each case:

1. Show the problem and observed work without COGNISECT; record the educator's
   unaided competing explanations and desired follow-up.
2. Run the case through COGNISECT.
3. Record approve, edit, reject, or abstain separately for proposed hypotheses
   and compiled probe.
4. Ask whether the prediction ledger and final note are understandable and usable.
5. Record friction, missing context, and unsafe wording without inserting the
   participant's identity into the application.

After six cases, choose one concrete product change supported by the session,
implement it, rerun the affected flow, and record whether the issue improved.

## Private session record

- Consent version and timestamp.
- Role category without public identity.
- Per-case unaided notes and decisions.
- Per-case hypothesis/probe approve-edit-reject-abstain outcomes.
- Ledger and final-note usability response.
- Selected product change, commit, rerun result, and unresolved concern.
- Separate public-use permissions and approved wording.

## Reporting rule

Report counts descriptively for this one usability session; do not generalize to
teachers, classrooms, learning, accuracy, time savings, or adoption. If the
session does not occur, remove every review-derived claim rather than substituting
an invented participant.
